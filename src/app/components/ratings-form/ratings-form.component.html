SMAFRS

1. Eating
0: Fully dependent.
1: Needs help for feeding for greater than 50% of the total amout.
2: Needs help for feeding for less than 50% of the total amount.
3: Independent but needs assisting device or modified food consistency or safety consideration.
4: Independent but requires longer time on a regular basis (regular food, regular utensils).
5: Complete independence (regular utensils,regular food and texture).

2. Dressing Upper Body.
0: Fully dependent.
1: Needs help but able to perform less than 50% of the task alone.
2: Needs help but able to perform greater than or equal to 50% of the task alone.
3: Modified independence (independent with regard to modification of clothes).
4: Partial independence (may need longer than reasonable time or help to get clothes from drawer or with buttons).
5: Complete independence.

3. Dressing lower body.
0: Fully dependent.
1: Needs help but able to perform less than 50% of the task alone.
2: Needs help but able to perform greater than or equal to 50% of the task alone.
3: Modified independence (independent with regard to modification of clothes).
4: Partial independence (may need longer than reasonable time or help to get clothes from drawer or with buttons).
5: Complete independence.

4. Bathing.
0: Fully dependent.
1: Needs help to perform most of the task (definitively >50%).
2: Needs help but able to perform greater than or equal to 50% of the task alone.
3: Modified independence (independent with regard to modification of clothes).
4: Partial independence (may need longer than reasonable time or help to get clothes from drawer or with buttons).
5: Complete independence.

5. Toileting.
0: Fully dependent.
1: Needs help for most of the task.
2: Needs help (supporting upper body, cleaning) for some of the task.
3: Modified independence (special equipment, extra time, safety considerations).
4: Partial independence (no need for help with toileting but needs help to adjust clothing before and after).
5: Complete independence (including adjusting clothing before and after).

6. Grooming and oral hygiene.
0: Fully dependent.
1: Needs help for part of the task.
2: Needs help for some of the task.
3: Modified independence (needs special orthoses, extra time, or safety considerations).
4: Partial independence (needs extra time to handle toothbrush or toothpaste).
5: Complete independence (including handling toothpaste).

7. Turning in bed and adjusting the bed clothes
0: Fully dependent.
1: Needs help to turn or adjusts sheets most of the time.
2: Needs help to turn or adjust sheets partly or some of the time.
3: Can turn alone or adjust sheets but with great difficulty.
4: Somewhat clumsy but no help needed.
5: Complete independence.

8 Transfers (from chair, toilet, bath, wheelchair)
0: Fully dependent.
1: Needs help and assistance for most of the task.
2: Needs help and assistance for some of the task.
3: Modified independence (able to perform task alone but needs supervision, some safety considerations, special orthoses).
4: Partial independence (able to perform task alone but slower, no safety considerations, no supervision needed).
5: Complete independence.

9. Walking
0: Non-ambulatory but may have functional leg movements.
1: Help to stand but cannot walk, or very little.
2: Help needed (able to walk with support after helped to a stand).
3: Ambulant (may need special orthoses, support, such as ankle foot orthosis, some safety considerations, direct supervision needed).
4: Ambulant but slower normal (mild Trendelenburg signs, no help needed, no need for direct supervision needed).
5: Ambulant, normal gait.

10 Climbing stairs
0: Non-ambulatory but may have functional leg movements.
1: Help to stand but cannot walk, or very little.
2: Help needed (able to walk with support after helped to a stand).
3: Ambulant (may need special orthoses, support, such as ankle foot orthosis, some safety considerations, direct supervision needed).
4: Ambulant but slower normal (mild Trendelenburg signs, no help needed, no need for direct supervision needed).
5: Ambulant, normal gait.
